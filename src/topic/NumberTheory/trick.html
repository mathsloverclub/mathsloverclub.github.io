Divide

Let $ a $ and $b$ be any two integers with $a \ne 0$. Then $a$ is said to divide $b$ if there is an integer $c$ such that $b= a c$.
Then we write $$a \mid b$$ e.g. $2 \mid 8$ because $8 = 2 \times 4$

Linear Diophantine Equations

A linear equation $ax+by =c$ with $a \ne 0$ and $b \ne 0$ is called a Linear Diophantine Equation in two unknowns $x$ and $y$. For example, $$\begin{aligned} 5x-4y &= 21 \\ 170x-455y &= 635\\ 256x + 1166y &= 4 \end{aligned}$$

Solving Linear Diophantine Equation

We can guess the solution of some linear Diophantine equation.
For example, $$5x- 4y = 21$$ has solutions $$ \begin{aligned} x = 5, \hspace{2em} y &= 1 \\ x = 1, \hspace{2em} y &= -4 \\ x = 9,\hspace{2em} y &= 6 \end{aligned} $$

Theorem

Let $a$ and $b$ be any non-zero integers and $d=(a,b)$. The linear Diophantine equation $ax+by=c$ has a solution if and only if $d \mid c$. If there is a solution, say $X$ and $Y$, then any other solution is the form of $x= X - \frac{b}{d}t$ and $y= Y - \frac{a}{d}t$, $t$ being an integer.

In previous example $5x- 4y = 21$, we have $a = 5$, $b=-4$ and $d=(a,b)=1$. Thus this theorem says that equation has solutions because $1 \mid c$ which is equal to $21$.

Solve $ 170x -455y = 625 $

Now let's try the problem more difficult to solve. First we have to check the given Diophantine Equation. To do so, we need to find $(170,455)$, the greatest common divisor of $170$ and $455$. If $(170, 455) \mid 625$, we know that the given Diophantine Equation has solution and we have to find it. To be able to find the soltion of Diophantine Equation, let's find $(170,455)$ in the following ways. $$\begin{aligned} 455 &= 170 \times 2 + 115 \\ 170 &= 115 \times 1 + 55\\ 115 &= 55 \times 2 +5 \\ 55 &= 5 \times 11 \end{aligned}$$ Now we get the g.c.d of $170$ and $455$, which is equal to $5$. Since $5 \mid 625$, we know that the given diophantine has solutions. To get the solution, find the expression of $5$ in terms of $455$ and $170$. This can be easily done by undoing the above equations. $$ \begin{aligned} 5 &= 115 - 55 \times 2 \\ 5 &= 115 - (170 - 115 \times 1) \times 2\\ 5 &= 115 - 170 \times 2 + 115 \times 2\\ 5 &= 170 \times (-2) + 115 \times 3\\ 5 &= 170 \times (-2) + (455 - 170 \times 2) \times 3\\ 5 &= 170 \times (-2) + 455 \times 3 + 170 \times (-6) \\ 5 &= 455 \times 3 + 170 \times (-8) \end{aligned} $$ Unfortunately, we are not finding the solution of $455 x + 170 y = 5$. But we are finding the solution of $455x+170y = 625$. So we have to multiply both sides of equation by $125$. And we get $$455 \times 375 + 170 \times (-1000) = 625$$ Thus $375$ and $-1000$ is the solution of the linear diophantine equation.
